I'm originally from Auckland, New Zealand. I moved to Boston in 2017 to pursue my PhD.

I earned my BS in Psychology and Exercise Science, and my MS in Psychology, from The University of Auckland. I completed my PhD in Psychology from Northeastern University in the Center for Cognitive and Brain Health, in the Summer of 2022.

I'm currently an Assistant Professor at the University of Rhode Island, and PI of the Logan Lab. Starting Fall 2023, the Logan Lab will be recruiting PhD students in Kinesiology or Neuroscience.

Alongside my research and teaching, I also enjoy all things nature, science, and sports. When I'm not in the lab, you can find me running, hiking mountains, practicing yoga, surfing, coaching water polo, or playing basketball.
